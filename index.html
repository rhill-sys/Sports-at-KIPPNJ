Why Sports

Every conversation parents have starts with sports. Here's why we're investing.

📈

Better Grades, Better Outcomes

Students who play organized sports have up to 40% higher test scores. 66% of A-students play organized sports vs. 24% of D/F students.

🧠

Physical & Mental Health

Active youth show higher self-esteem, lower depression, and reduced risk of obesity. Research shows team sports can be as effective as medication for long-term mental health.

📱

Productive After-School Time

Kids need somewhere to go after school. Sports get them off their phones and into structured, team-based programs where they're moving, competing, and building relationships.

🏆

School Culture & Pride

Athletic programs create shared identity, bring families into school life, and give students something to rally around. Sports are a retention tool.

💰

This Is a Social Justice Issue

Low-income kids play organized sports at half the rate of wealthier peers. Competitive youth sports cost thousands of dollars a year, locking out the families we serve.

🚀

A Replicable Model

If this works, it's a proof of concept for how any public school system can build athletic pathways for low-income students — from elementary rec leagues to college commitments.

🎓

Character & Leadership

Confidence, teamwork, hard work, discipline. The same values we teach in classrooms are reinforced on courts and fields.

💼

College & Career Pathways

In the age of AI, sports is a growing, human-centric industry. College scholarships, coaching, agents, physical therapy, sports marketing — real career paths for our students.

Current State

Where we are today across KIPP NJ schools.

Elementary School

Little organized sports programming. PE classes exist but few after-school rec leagues, clinics, or structured sport exposure. This is the biggest gap.

Middle School

Basketball teams at all schools. Soccer teams at about half our schools. Some schools have volleyball. No systematic approach — each school decides what to offer.

High School

NCA, KHS, and Lab all compete in NJSIAA. Sports include soccer, basketball, volleyball, track, cross country, wrestling, and cheer. Soccer: NCA was strong in a low division, KHS was weak. Lab has everything except soccer.

The Vision

Focus on sports our kids play, at the age they need to get started.

Elementary (K-4)

Soccer & Basketball First

Start early with the two sports that matter most in our communities.

  • PE curriculum includes soccer and basketball units
  • After-school rec programs 2-3 days/week
  • Small-sided games, clinics, fun-first approach
  • Both sports require early exposure to develop skills
  • Minimal equipment needs — accessible and affordable
Middle School (5-8)

School-Based Teams

Every middle school fields competitive teams.

  • Soccer and basketball teams at every campus
  • Each MS will have a volleyball team
  • League play and inter-school competition
  • Talent identification for academy/travel programs
High School (9-12)

NJSIAA Competition

Full varsity programs across NCA, KHS, and Lab.

  • Soccer, basketball, volleyball, track, XC, wrestling, cheer
  • Combined teams across schools where needed (soccer: NCA + Lab)
  • Freshman, JV, and varsity squads
  • College recruitment pipeline (Hudl, showcases)
  • State championship contention as programs mature

The Principle

Build from the bottom up. Best systems start early.

Features of Best Youth Sports Systems

Early elementary: broad exposure through rec programs. Grades 2-4: travel programs start. Grades 5-12: elite club programs. Year-round training. Professional coaching at every level.

Where KIPP Is Now

Pockets of excellence, but uneven. HS NJSIAA programs exist, MS has teams but quality varies, and ES is mostly empty. The goal is to fill the pipeline so that by the time kids reach high school, they've had years of development.

⚽ Soccer

A free K-12 soccer program for KIPP NJ students — building a model for how public schools can upend pay-to-play.

Why Soccer

⚽

Deep Community Roots

Newark, Paterson, and Camden are home to large Latino, Brazilian, Haitian, and West African families for whom soccer is central to neighborhood life — and thousands more who would play if given early exposure.

🏆

World Cup 2026

The World Cup is at MetLife Stadium — in our backyard. A once-in-a-generation moment to connect our students to the biggest sporting event on earth.

📈

Fastest-Growing Sport

Soccer is the #1 most popular sport globally and one of the fastest-growing in the US, especially in our communities. Equipment needs are minimal.

K-12 Soccer Pathway

K-2

Foundation & Love of the Game

Soccer integrated into PE. After-school clubs. Small-sided games, no positions, all fun. Every participant gets a ball to take home.

Grades 3-4

Skill Building & Identification

After-school clubs expand. Intramural leagues with tournaments. Talent identification begins — Etiennes conduct quarterly player ID sessions across all campuses.

Grades 6-8

Competition & System

All KIPP middle schools field boys' and girls' teams. Unified playing style designed by the Etiennes. Winter futsal. Open tryouts bring non-KIPP kids in.

Grades 9-12

NJSIAA Competition

Combined teams bringing together NCA and Lab players with freshman, JV, and varsity squads. Hudl highlight film for every junior and senior.

Academy

KIPP Soccer Academy

Top players from KIPP schools across Newark and Paterson. Year-round competitive training. EDP travel-league play (U8-U18). College showcases. Separate 501(c)(3). Scaling to 22 teams by Year 4.

Other Sports

Volleyball, track, cross country, wrestling, cheer, and more. This is the baseline alignment — individual schools can add other sports as they wish.

High School Sports (NJSIAA)

All three KIPP NJ high schools — NCA, KHS, and Lab — compete in NJSIAA across multiple sports.

🏐

Volleyball

NJSIAA competition at all three high schools. Some middle schools also have volleyball teams — expanding to all MS campuses over time.

🏃

Track & Field

NJSIAA competition at NCA, KHS, and Lab. Indoor and outdoor seasons. Minimal equipment requirements make this accessible across all campuses.

🏃

Cross Country

NJSIAA fall season sport. Requires no specialized facilities — any campus can field a team. Builds endurance and mental toughness.

🥊

Wrestling

NJSIAA winter sport at KIPP NJ high schools. Individual competition builds discipline, grit, and accountability in ways team sports don't always reach.

📣

Cheer

Competition cheer at KIPP NJ high schools. Builds school spirit, creates performance opportunities, and develops athleticism, teamwork, and confidence.

Expansion Priorities

Where we're growing next.

Volleyball → Middle School

Already at some MS campuses. Each middle school will have a volleyball team, creating a feeder for HS programs.

Other MS Sports — TBD

As infrastructure and coaching capacity grow, we'll evaluate adding more sports at the middle school level. School leaders who want to start something earlier are encouraged to go for it.
