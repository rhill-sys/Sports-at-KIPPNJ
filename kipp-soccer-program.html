Why Soccer, Why Now

The talent is here. The infrastructure to develop it has not been.

⚽

Deep Community Roots

Newark, Paterson, and Camden are home to large Latino, Brazilian, Haitian, and West African families for whom soccer is central to neighborhood life — and thousands more who would play if given early exposure.

🏆

World Cup 2026

The World Cup is at MetLife Stadium — in our backyard. A once-in-a-generation moment to connect our students to the biggest sporting event on earth.

📈

Proven Impact on Kids

Studies show student-athletes have higher proficiency scores and stronger mental health.

📱

Productive After-School Time

Kids need somewhere to go after school. Soccer gets them off their phones and into structured, team-based programs where they're moving, competing, and building relationships with adults and peers.

💰

Upending Pay-to-Play

Competitive youth soccer costs thousands of dollars a year, making it inaccessible to most kids in our communities.

🚀

A Replicable Model

If this works, it's a proof of concept for how any public school system can build elite athletic pathways for low-income students.

The K-12 Pathway

A child entering any KIPP school in kindergarten has access to soccer programming every year through graduation. The pathway serves every student — not just future varsity players.

K-2

Foundation & Love of the Game

Soccer integrated into PE 2 days/week. After-school clubs 2 days/week. Small-sided games (3v3, 4v4), no positions, no tactics, all fun. Every participant gets a ball to take home. Target: 60%+ student participation.

Grades 3-4

Skill Building & Identification

After-school clubs expand to 3 days/week. Intramural leagues with quarterly tournaments. 5v5 and 7v7 formats. Talent identification begins — Etiennes conduct quarterly player ID sessions across all campuses. Top players invited to academy development sessions.

Grades 6-8

Competition & System Installation

All KIPP middle schools field boys' and girls' teams. Training 3-4x/week + 1-2 games/week. Unified playing style installed — same formation, same philosophy, designed by the Etiennes. Winter futsal is a core development period. Open tryouts bring talented non-KIPP kids into the system.

Grades 9-12

Championship Pursuit & College Pipeline

NJSIAA competition — Newark in North II (Group III), Camden in South Jersey. Combined teams bringing together NCA and Lab players with freshman, JV, and varsity squads. 5 training sessions/week in season. Hudl highlight film for every junior and senior. Target: 5+ college placements per graduating class by Year 5.

Academy

KIPP Soccer Academy (501(c)(3))

Pulling top players from KIPP schools across Newark and Paterson. Year-round competitive training. EDP travel-league play (U8-U18). College showcases with 200+ coaches. Futsal in winter. Elite coaching from the Etiennes. Free for every student. Separate nonprofit structure. Scaling to 22 teams and 350-400 players by Year 4.
